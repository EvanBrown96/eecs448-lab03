Born in Salzburg, I showed prodigious ability from my earliest childhood. Already competent on keyboard and violin, I composed from the age of five and performed before European royalty. At 17, I was engaged as a musician at the Salzburg court, but grew restless and traveled in search of a better position. While visiting Vienna in 1781, I was dismissed from his Salzburg position. I chose to stay in the capital, where I achieved fame but little financial security. During my final years in Vienna, I composed many of my best-known symphonies, concertos, and operas, and portions of the Requiem, which was largely unfinished at the time of my early death at the age of 35. The circumstances of my death have been much mythologized.
